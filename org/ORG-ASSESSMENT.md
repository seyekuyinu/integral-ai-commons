# Organization Assessment

How to know if the human-centered AI operating model is actually working.

This is not a compliance checklist. It is a set of honest questions your organization returns to regularly — to catch drift before it becomes damage, and to recognize growth before it gets taken for granted.

Run this assessment at 30 days, 90 days, and every six months after that.

**Time:** 60 minutes
**Who participates:** The same people who did the onboarding session
**Format:** Conversation, not survey — though a pre-session survey can help surface things people won't say out loud

---

## The four measures

Everything in this assessment traces back to four things. These come from the framework's core metrics. For each one, you are asking: are we moving in the right direction?

| Measure | The question |
|---|---|
| Capacity expansion | Are our people more capable than they were before? |
| Equity of access | Is AI use closing gaps in our organization or widening them? |
| Agency preservation | Are humans still making the decisions that matter? |
| Community legitimacy | Do the people we serve trust and recognize themselves in how we work? |

---

## Section 1 — Capacity expansion

*Are our people more capable than they were before?*

### Indicators to look for

**Positive signs:**
- People can explain what AI is doing in their work and why
- People are taking on work they couldn't do before — not just doing existing work faster
- People are getting better at prompting, directing, and evaluating AI output over time
- New team members onboard faster because AI handles routine tasks and frees time for learning

**Warning signs:**
- People can produce output but can't explain the reasoning behind it
- Skills that existed before AI adoption are atrophying — writing, analysis, judgment calls
- People feel less confident in their own work without AI assistance
- When AI tools are unavailable, work stops rather than slows

### Questions to ask the room

1. Think about something you did with AI this month. Could you have done it without AI? If not — is that because AI genuinely extended your capability, or because you've stopped practicing that skill?

2. Is there anything you used to be able to do that you're less confident about now?

3. What have you learned about your own work by working alongside AI?

## Section 2 — Equity of access

*Is AI use closing gaps or widening them?*

### Indicators to look for

**Positive signs:**
- AI tools are used across the team, not just by the most technically confident people
- People who were previously bottlenecked by writing, research, or admin work are unblocked
- AI is helping surface ideas and contributions from people who were previously quieter
- No one feels left behind or surveilled by AI adoption

**Warning signs:**
- AI fluency is concentrating power in the hands of a few team members
- Some team members are using AI extensively while others haven't been supported to start
- AI is being used to monitor or evaluate team members without their knowledge or consent
- Customers or communities with less technical literacy are receiving worse service

### Questions to ask the room

1. Who on the team is getting the most benefit from AI tools right now? Who is getting the least? Why?

2. Is anyone using AI in ways that affect their colleagues without those colleagues knowing?

3. Are there customers or community members who are receiving a different quality of service because of how we've integrated AI? Is that difference equitable?

---

## Section 3 — Agency preservation

*Are humans still making the decisions that matter?*

### Indicators to look for

**Positive signs:**
- The non-delegables list is being honored — decisions on it are still being made by humans
- People feel ownership over their work even when AI contributed significantly
- When AI produces something wrong, people catch it — they're still paying attention
- Disagreement with AI output is normal and comfortable, not rare or awkward

**Warning signs:**
- Decisions that were on the non-delegables list are quietly being handed to AI
- People are accepting AI output without review because it feels faster
- When AI makes a mistake, it goes unnoticed until a customer or stakeholder catches it
- People feel uncomfortable overriding AI recommendations even when their judgment says to

### Questions to ask the room

1. Look at the non-delegables list from your `ORG-SETUP.md`. Has anything on that list migrated to AI without a conscious decision to move it?

2. In the last month, when did you override an AI recommendation? What happened?

3. Is there anywhere in your work where you feel like you're rubber-stamping AI output rather than genuinely reviewing it?

---

## Section 4 — Community legitimacy

*Do the people we serve trust and recognize themselves in how we work?*

### Indicators to look for

**Positive signs:**
- Customer or community feedback reflects that interactions still feel personal and human
- People we serve know when they're interacting with AI and have chosen to
- No one has raised concerns about AI use in our communications or services
- The communities we serve see themselves reflected in how we use AI

**Warning signs:**
- Customer complaints about generic, impersonal, or off-brand communication
- People discovering AI involvement in interactions they assumed were human
- Communities raising concerns about how their data or context is being used
- Our AI output reflects assumptions that don't fit the people we serve

### Questions to ask the room

1. Has anyone — customer, community member, partner — raised a concern about how we're using AI? What was it?

2. Is there anything in how we communicate now that wouldn't have passed our own standard six months ago?

3. If the people we serve could see exactly how we use AI in our work, what would we be proud of? What would we be uncomfortable about?

## Scoring

This assessment does not produce a score. Scores create the wrong incentive — optimizing for the number rather than for the reality it's supposed to represent.

Instead, end each section with a simple verdict:

**Moving forward** — We are doing better than we were. Name one specific thing that's improved.

**Holding steady** — We haven't regressed but we haven't grown. Name one thing worth pushing on.

**Needs attention** — Something here is drifting in the wrong direction. Name it specifically and decide what to do about it.

---

## After the assessment

**If things are moving forward:**
Acknowledge it concretely. Tell the team what's working. Good operating models fail because the maintenance feels invisible — make the progress visible.

**If something needs attention:**
Don't treat it as a failure. Treat it as the assessment working. The point of the tool is to catch drift early. Name the issue, assign an owner, set a date to revisit.

**Update your documents:**
If the assessment revealed that your non-delegables list needs revision, revise it. If your `ORG-CLAUDE.md` no longer reflects how you actually work, update it. A document no one updates is a document no one trusts.

---

## The long arc

At twelve months, add one more question to the assessment:

> Are we the kind of organization that uses AI well?

Not just efficiently. Not just safely. Well — in the sense that our AI use reflects our values, serves our community, and leaves the humans in our organization more capable and more themselves than before.

That is the standard. Everything else is a proxy for it.
